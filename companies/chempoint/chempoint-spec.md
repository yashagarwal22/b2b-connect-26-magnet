## 0. The spine (read this first)

Everything below hangs on one insight about ChemPoint's business, drawn straight from the Account 360.

**ChemPoint does not sell chemicals. It sells demand creation and trust.** In its own words, it is "a marketing and sales firm with full distribution capabilities, a service company for chemical producers with a distribution backbone." Producers like 3M, BASF, Dow, Eastman and IFF hand ChemPoint exclusive line mandates so ChemPoint can find buyers, answer their technical questions, and earn the order. The distribution is the backbone. The product is being found and being trusted.

That is exactly the function AI answer engines are now intermediating.

Two-thirds of senior B2B decision-makers already use AI to research suppliers. AI referral traffic grew 357% year on year and converts about 31% higher than organic search. The buyer's first question about a coating additive or a food-grade emulsifier increasingly goes to ChatGPT or Perplexity, not to Google and not to a rep. Whoever the answer engine cites wins the moment of intent.

So the strategic question for ChemPoint is not "should we add AI." It is "when a buyer asks an AI for the right specialty chemical, does it cite ChemPoint, or does it cite BASF directly." If it cites the producer, the demand-creation service ChemPoint is paid to provide gets routed around at the exact moment it matters, and producer-direct disintermediation stops being a slow threat and becomes an instant one.

ChemPoint already shipped the right instinct. **Ask ChemPoint went live June 1, 2026.** The work now is not a better model. It is grounding that answer layer in the one asset producers and Brenntag cannot copy: 25 years of technical answers, exclusive-line data, and the buyer relationships only ChemPoint owns. Do that, and ordinary AI defends the core business. Skip it, and the best-funded chatbot in the category still loses the citation.

That spine runs through the thesis, the wedge, every module, and every play.

## 2. Voice rules (the bar for every line)

Hold every line to these. They are the difference between advisory content and AI slop.

- **Aphoristic, contrastive openers.** "A copilot sells the tool. An autopilot sells the work."
- **A name on every claim.** Grainger, Watts, Brenntag, BASF, Dawn Foods, Sonepar. No floating assertions.
- **One load-bearing stat per idea.** A single number, dropped where it lands hardest. Never a wall of figures.
- **Reframes that flip intuition.** "You'll be sold a $3M transformation. Your problem is a six-week fix."
- **Economy.** One to three sentence paragraphs. If a word can go with no loss, cut it.
- **No AI tells.** No em-dashes. Periods, colons, commas only. En-dashes live in numeric ranges ($5–18M).
- **It is about them.** Lead with what ChemPoint owns and what the competitor cannot copy.

| Write this | Not this |
|---|---|
| "The model is the cheapest thing in the room." | "AI represents a transformative opportunity." |
| "Margin you already negotiated, leaking." | "Pricing optimization can drive significant value." |
| "Watts made Perplexity cite it over rivals." | "Leading manufacturers have seen gains." |

**Device:** a four-phase timeline. Phase 1 is the wedge and is lit "active." Each phase funds the next. Sequence is data → workflow → AI → agents, and the fast, low-data-prerequisite win goes first.

 [ACTIVE]

**Phase 1, starts now. Storefront: ground Ask ChemPoint, turn on AEO.**
Make every Ask ChemPoint answer spec-accurate from an enriched PIM, then feed that same data to ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google AI so they cite ChemPoint. It runs on content you own and lands inside Kelley's first 90 days. This is the wedge.

**Phase 2, 3 to 6 months. Sales and marketing: convert the demand you create.**
Wire 6sense and ClickDimensions intent, on-site behavior and AEO traffic into next-best-action for inside sales. Add B2B-aware search and cross-sell propensity to defend the base. This is your core engine. Make it AI-native.

**Phase 3, 6 to 12 months. Pricing, personalization and marketplace.**
Account-based pricing, entitlement and behavioral personalization across the storefront and IngredientPoint. These are your stated Marketplace and Personalization priorities, wired through Salesforce and TradeCentric.

**Phase 4, back office and the agentic bet. Demand sensing, supplier risk, agentic readiness.**

*Why this order.* Back-office before front-office is the usual rule, but ChemPoint is a demand-creation firm with a live answer engine and a producer-disintermediation clock. The defensible, start-now revenue is at the front, so the front goes first and self-funds the data and operations work behind it.
