## Voice rules (the bar for every line)

The benchmark is Sequoia's *Services: The New Software* — a whole argument carried by one axis
and a handful of load-bearing facts. Hold every word to that.

- **Aphoristic openers.** Each module opens with a short, contrastive couplet.
  *"A copilot sells the tool. An autopilot sells the work."*
- **Named examples, immediately.** No claim without a name pinned to it (Grainger, Watts, Brenntag, BASF).
- **One load-bearing stat per idea.** A single number, dropped where it lands hardest. Never a wall of stats.
- **Reframes that flip intuition.** *"You'll be sold a $3M transformation. Your problem is a six-week fix."*
- **Economy.** 1–3 sentence paragraphs. If a word can be cut with no loss, cut it.
- **No AI tells.** No em-dashes (`—`). Use periods, colons, commas. En-dashes only in numeric ranges (`$4–14M`).
- **It's about them, not us.** Lead with what ChemPoint owns and what the competitor can't copy.

**Do / Don't**

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "The model is the cheapest thing in the room." | "AI represents a transformative opportunity." |
| "Margin you already negotiated, leaking." | "Pricing optimization can drive significant value." |
| "Watts made Perplexity cite it over rivals." | "Leading manufacturers have seen gains." |
