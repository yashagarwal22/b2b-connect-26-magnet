## 0. The spine (read this first)

Everything below hangs on one insight about Thermo Fisher's business, drawn straight from the Account 360.

**Thermo Fisher does not have a storefront problem. It has a buying-experience that is splintered across five-plus procurement networks.** A scientist who wants a reagent or an Orbitrap mass spec can land on the Adobe AEM storefront, or punch in through Coupa, SAP Ariba, Jaggaer, Oracle, cc-Hubwoo or Science Warehouse. The 1M+ SKU catalog is one of the most defensible assets in life science. The way a customer reaches it is six front doors that do not talk to each other.

That fragmentation lands at the worst possible moment.

Scientists and the AI agents working for them are starting their supplier search inside an AI assistant, not on Google and not on thermofisher.com. Gartner expects 90% of B2B purchases to be AI-intermediated by 2028. The moat is 25 years of life-science technical content, 1M SKUs, and the punchout and supply relationships only Thermo Fisher owns. The risk is that the catalog is world-class and the customer never reaches it through the right door.

The reframe is the whole spec. The marketplace and punchout sprawl is not a plumbing problem to hand to IT. It is the buying experience, and consolidating it does two things at once: it makes ordering simpler for the customer, and it strips procurement friction that quietly offsets the tariff and FX drag now pressing on margin. AI's role here is not a chatbot. It is unifying the procurement experience and making Thermo Fisher the cited answer when a researcher asks an AI for a reagent or an instrument.

The honest guardrail runs through the close. The storefront is Adobe AEM, not Microsoft Dynamics Commerce. So i95Dev's angle is surgical commerce and marketplace integration, AEO, and personalization services on the storefront Thermo Fisher already runs. It is not a D365 ERP replacement and it is not a platform rip-out. This is consolidation and integration. The buyer said it plainly on the conference floor: "Marketplace providers such as Ariba, Jaggaer, Salesforce."

That spine runs through the thesis, the wedge, every module, and every play.
