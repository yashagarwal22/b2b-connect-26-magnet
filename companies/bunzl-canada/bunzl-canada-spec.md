## 0. The spine (read this first)

Everything below hangs on one insight about Bunzl Canada's business, drawn straight from the Account 360.

**The group already proved the model. Canada has not run the play yet.** Digital is 76% of Bunzl plc orders, and Europe runs a sophisticated unified platform: BunzlOne, an Adobe Commerce storefront on a Mirakl marketplace engine, with punchout and customer-specific pricing. But that platform is the EU/group build across 60-plus subsidiaries. Bunzl Canada, serving 45,000-plus business customers, appears to still run its own customer-ordering experience on Microsoft Dynamics 365 Sales plus a WordPress marketing layer.

That gap is the whole opportunity. The parent has flagged North American execution issues, and Canada is consolidating 15 warehouses down to 6. The storefront decision for Canada is still open. The champion floated "Shopify," but no migration has happened.

The moat is real and it is Canadian: 45,000 recurring business customers, roughly 30% private-label penetration through REGARD and EcoSystems, and replenishment that repeats every cycle. Sysco and TENAQUIP want those accounts. Distribution-and-Dynamics is exactly i95Dev's wheelhouse.

So the strategic question is not "should Canada copy BunzlOne." It is "when Canada picks a customer-ordering storefront, does it ride the Infor SX.enterprise and Dynamics 365 backbone Canada already runs, made into a contained decision now while the decision is open, or does a wrong platform bet get locked in first." Answer it, and every one of the 45,000 replenishment relationships compounds on systems competitors cannot copy. Miss it, and the most defensible base in Canadian distribution orders through a marketing site, not a commerce platform.

GUARDRAIL: do not assume Shopify. Shape the platform decision objectively, and lead with integration to the Infor and D365 backbone Canada already runs. That spine runs through the thesis, the wedge, every module, and every play.
