## 0. The spine (read this first)

Everything below hangs on one honest insight about Terumo, drawn straight from the Account 360.

**Terumo is scaling its GS26 strategy and shopping for software to track it, and the tools on its shortlist are only as good as the data flowing into them, which is the one place i95Dev actually helps.** The attendee, Mohit Sheera, is a Strategic Planning Leader evaluating OKR and project-portfolio platforms: Planview, Quantive, WorkBoard, Sopheon and similar. That is not a commerce or ERP need, and it is candidly not what i95Dev sells.

What is real is the layer underneath. Those platforms assume the operational and financial data flowing into them is clean and connected. For a global medtech mid-GS26, pushing double-digit growth across the US and China with localized manufacturing, that data is usually fragmented across entities. A strategy-execution tool fed by stale or manual inputs reports a fiction confidently.

So the honest counsel is to separate the two decisions. Buy the OKR or PPM platform if it is needed; that is Terumo's call and not i95Dev's product. But do not expect it to fix inputs that are siloed. That is a data problem, and it is the cheaper, more durable thing to solve first.

The strategic question for Terumo is therefore narrow and candid. Does the strategy-execution platform report reality, or stale numbers dressed up in a nicer dashboard. For a company betting GS26 on growth and localization, the data underneath decides whether the tool is worth buying.

That is where AI and i95Dev actually pay here. Not the OKR tool itself. It is the BI and data foundation under it, on the SAP base Terumo already runs. The model and the dashboard are commodities. The connected, governed data spanning devices, consumables and global operations is the asset no tool can hand you.

That spine runs through the thesis, the wedge, every module, and every play. Guardrail: this buyer's need is outside i95Dev's core, so the honest angle is the data foundation, and the account is a low-priority nurture.
