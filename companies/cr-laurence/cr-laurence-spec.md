## 0. The spine (read this first)

Everything below hangs on one insight about CRL, and the buyer's own named stack gives it away.

**CRL wins on the deepest glazing-and-hardware catalog in the world, built since 1920, and it has already bought the tools to search and personalize it. The one thing it has not bought is the layer that makes AI engines cite it.** Elisabeth Ducharme, Global eCommerce Lead, came asking for "AI, live chat, on-site search solutions, AEO solutions." But her named stack is SAP S/4, Algolia, Dynamic Yield and Braze. She already owns search and personalization. The real gap in her own wishlist is AEO, the one almost no building-products distributor does.

That changes the whole pitch. The move is not to sell a second search engine to a company that runs Algolia, or a personalization tool to one that runs Dynamic Yield. It is the answer layer on top of what she owns: structured, machine-readable spec data so ChatGPT and Perplexity cite CRL, and account-aware ranking that makes the Algolia she already runs work harder.

The urgency is real and underpriced. AI engines drove 1.13B referral visits in June 2025, up 357% year over year, and those buyers reach whoever the engine cites. Watts Water already gets cited by Perplexity over competitors on selection content. Almost no glazing distributor is doing this, which makes it a window, not a race.

So the strategic question for CRL is whether the next digital spend buys more of what it already has, or the answer layer that captures the demand now forming in AI engines. For a catalog this deep, getting cited is the cheapest growth on the board.

That is where AI actually pays here. Not a second search engine. Not a live-chat bot that cannot read a spec. It is AEO, spec-grounded RAG, and account-aware ranking on the Algolia and Dynamic Yield CRL already runs. The model is the commodity. The deepest glazing catalog and its specs, built since 1920, are the moat.

That spine runs through the thesis, the wedge, every module, and every play. Guardrail: do not sell a tool CRL already owns; sell the layer on top and the commerce-to-SAP bridge. PE cost discipline favors surgical, self-funding wins.

- *ROI:* Every shift from rep to digital recovers margin and time.
- *What i95Dev does:* Visual and part-number search on the catalog, integrated to inventory. *(Verified. Grainger.)*

### Commerce and the foundation

**D. Account-aware ranking and pricing on existing tools.**
- *Problem:* Algolia and Dynamic Yield run on behavior, not on the account's contract price and approved items.
- *Named case:* The B2B rule is that contract pricing and entitlements are the rails any AI must respect.
- *ROI:* More self-service and trust on the accounts that matter, without a new tool.
- *What i95Dev does:* Account-aware ranking and pricing layered on Algolia and Dynamic Yield, bridged to SAP S/4. *(Directional. Research dump, Part 3.3.)*
