## 0. The spine (read this first)

Everything below hangs on one insight about ADI's situation, drawn straight from the Account 360.

**ADI is doing three hard things at once, and they all converge on one platform.** It is integrating the $1.4B Snap One acquisition, migrating its ERP from Epicor Prophet 21 to Microsoft Dynamics 365 ("Project Avengers"), and spinning off from Resideo into a standalone $4.8B public company on the NYSE by H2 2026. Three programs, one deadline, one backbone.

The single most important fact for i95Dev: the active D365 ERP migration has no publicly named systems integrator, and there are two un-merged storefronts. The new ERP is Microsoft Dynamics 365 across the EU and NA. The commerce layer is one Optimizely Configured Commerce site for ADI and a separate, un-merged snapav.com for Snap One. This is the strongest Dynamics fit in the room and a direct whitespace.

The moat is the network. 200+ branches, Pro Rewards loyalty, and proprietary brands (Control4, Episode, Araknis) that no competitor carries. The proof the digital channel matters: web-influenced revenue runs 3x cart revenue, 44% of customers already buy digitally, and 70%+ engage the web or app. The contractor designs the system online and buys across the counter, so the digital layer steers the whole relationship.

The reframe: the spin-off is not a distraction from the platform work. It is the forcing function and the funding event for exactly the D365-plus-commerce unification i95Dev does. A standalone company on the NYSE must stand up its own unified platform before Investor Day, and the budget moment is now.

So the strategic question is not "should ADI add AI to the website." It is "when the standalone company stands up its platform, does the order run on one D365-wired storefront that carries ADI and Snap One together, or on two un-merged sites and an ERP migration with no SI of record." Answer it, and every one of the 200+ branches compounds on a system competitors cannot copy. Miss it, and the spin-off ships with two storefronts and a half-wired backbone. GUARDRAIL: lead with the D365 migration whitespace and storefront unification. This is the one account where i95Dev's core Dynamics capability is the headline, not a hedge.

That spine runs through the thesis, the wedge, every module, and every play.
