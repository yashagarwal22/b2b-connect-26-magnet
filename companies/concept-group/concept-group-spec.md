## 0. The spine (read this first)

Everything below hangs on one insight, drawn straight from the Account 360.

**Concept Group makes the best vacuum insulation on earth, and it is the only company that can.** Insulon is patented "shaped vacuum" geometry that conducts about 60x less heat than nylon, with walls down to 0.25mm. Founder Aarne Reid holds the USPTO patents. No competitor can ship it. That is a moat most companies would trade everything for.

The market is finally arriving. A US Department of Energy voucher put Concept Group on cryo-compressed hydrogen storage with Lawrence Livermore. Quantum computing needs cryogenic qubit cooling. Semiconductor sub-fab needs extreme thermal isolation. These are not someday markets. They are inflecting now, and they are exactly what Insulon was built for.

So the risk is not the product. The risk is that a once-in-a-decade demand inflection is arriving at a go-to-market that still quotes by hand.

Concept Group is a lean team. Demand creation is education-led and manual. The "Request a Quote" path is a human email thread. Every frontier program that sits in an inbox for six weeks is a multi-year custom contract drifting toward inertia or a faster competitor in an adjacent material.

Two things have changed that turn this from a small-company constraint into a rare opening. First, the buyer for a hydrogen transfer line or a qubit cooling assembly now starts at an AI answer engine, and 66% of senior UK B2B decision-makers already research suppliers that way, with the US following. Second, a company with singular IP can become the cited, default answer for an entire emerging category, because no rival can credibly contest the claim. Almost no industrial maker is structuring its content to win that citation yet.

The strategic question for Concept Group is therefore narrow and winnable. When an engineer asks an AI for the right thermal solution for liquid hydrogen or a cryostat, does it name Insulon. And when that engineer is ready, does the quote take six weeks or six minutes. Own the answer, automate the quote, and a 20-person company owns a frontier category in the AI-discovery era. That spine runs through the thesis, the wedge, every module, and every play.

## 2. Voice rules (the bar for every line)

Hold every line to these. They are the difference between advisory content and AI slop.

- **Aphoristic, contrastive openers.** "You don't have a marketing problem. You have a discovery problem."
- **A name on every claim.** Grainger, Watts, Schneider, Mirion, Lawrence Livermore. No floating assertions.
- **One load-bearing stat per idea.** A single number, dropped where it lands hardest. Never a wall of figures.
- **Reframes that flip intuition.** "The patent is the easy part. Being the answer is the work."
- **Economy.** One to three sentence paragraphs. If a word can go with no loss, cut it.
- **No AI tells.** No em-dashes. Periods, colons, commas only. En-dashes live in numeric ranges ($1–3M).
- **It is about them.** Lead with what Concept Group owns and what no competitor can copy.

| Write this | Not this |
|---|---|
| "The only company that can make it should be the one AI names." | "AI presents a transformative growth opportunity." |
| "A frontier contract, lost to an inbox." | "Quote automation can drive operational efficiency." |
| "Watts made Perplexity cite it over rivals." | "Leading manufacturers are seeing strong results." |

## 11. The roadmap (sequence it so it self-funds)

**Device:** a three-phase timeline, right-sized for an SMB. Phase 1 builds the capacity to deliver demand, then turns on the discovery that creates it, so the team is never flooded by leads it cannot quote. Phase 1 is lit "active."

```
 NOW ───────────── 3–6 MO ───────────── 6–12 MO
  ●                    ○                     ○
 Phase 1               Phase 2               Phase 3
 [ACTIVE]
 Quote-to-order +      Turn on AEO,          Nurture, portal,
 own the answer        capture intent        AI content scale
```

**Phase 1, starts now. Capacity and the category answer.**
Stand up guided quote-to-order on WooCommerce with PIM-backed dimensional SKUs so the lean team stops quoting by hand, and in parallel structure the existing technical content so AI engines cite Insulon. Capacity first, so the demand the answer layer creates has somewhere to land. This is the wedge plus its companion.

**Phase 2, 3 to 6 months. Convert the demand you create.**
Activate HubSpot and Salesforce nurture and lead scoring on the inbound you already attract, route Dealfront-identified engineer visits to named follow-up, and add RAG technical discovery so a buyer gets a spec-accurate answer on site. This turns anonymous frontier traffic into qualified pipeline.

**Phase 3, 6 to 12 months. Portal, personalization and content scale.**
Add an account portal for repeat and program customers, on-site personalization for the markets you are entering, and AI-assisted technical content so a small team publishes at the cadence the category requires. These compound the wedge without adding headcount.

*Why this order.* For a lean team the usual "demand first" instinct backfires: AEO that floods a manual quote desk just creates a backlog and a worse buyer experience. Build the capacity to deliver, then open the tap. The sequence protects the founder's time and the brand at the same moment it grows pipeline.
